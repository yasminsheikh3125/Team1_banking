BIVARIATE DATA :-

1.Churn VS Age:
• Churn means customers leaving the bank.
• Older customers tend to leave more often. This may be because they:
• Have different financial needs
• Prefer other banks with better services
• Middle-aged customers (around 30–40) are more stable and less likely to leave.
• This group is usually the most important customer segment for the bank.

Conclusion: Banks should focus on keeping older customers and maintaining strong relationships with middle-aged customers.

2.Churn vs Balance:
• Customers with high account balance are more likely to leave.
• Reasons:
-They are financially aware.
-They look for better offers, interest rates, or services.
• Other banks try to attract them with special benefits.
• Customers with low balance usually stay because: 
-They have fewer options.
- They are less active in switching banks.

Conclusion: Banks should give special attention and offers to high-balance customers to retain them.

3.Churn vs Number of Products:
• Customers with only 1 product (like just a savings account) are more likely to leave.
• Customers with multiple products (loans, cards, insurance, etc.) are less likely to leave because:
-They are more connected to the bank.
-Switching becomes difficult and inconvenient.
• The more products a customer uses, the stronger their relationship with the bank.

Conclusion: Banks should encourage customers to use more products to reduce churn.

4.Churn vs Active Members:
• Active members are customers who regularly use bank services (transactions, app usage, cards, etc.).
• Inactive members are those who rarely use their accounts.
• Customers who are inactive are much more likely to leave (churn) because:
- They are not engaged with the bank.
- They may already be using other banks.
- They don’t see much value in staying.
• On the other hand, active customers:
-Use multiple services.
-Have regular transactions.
-Feel more connected to the bank.
→ So, they are less likely to leave.

Conclusion:Increasing customer activity (through offers, apps, rewards) can reduce churn significantly.

5.Churn vs Country:
• France:
-Has the highest number of customers.
-Churn rate is average (medium risk).
• Germany:
-Customers are more likely to leave.
-Indicates high churn risk.
• Spain:
-Customers are more loyal.
-Very low churn rate.
•Overall, location affects customer behavior.

• Conclusion:
Germany = High risk
France = Medium risk
Spain = Low risk
• Banks should apply different strategies for each country.

6.Churn vs Gender:
• Both male and female customers mostly stay, meaning churn is generally low for both.
• Female customers:
-Have a slightly higher churn rate.
-May be more sensitive to service quality, trust, or experience.
• Male customers:
-Make up a larger portion of total customers.
-Slightly lower churn compared to females.
• However, the difference is very small, so:
-Gender is not a major factor.
-It has only a minor impact on churn.

Conclusion:Banks should not rely heavily on gender-based strategies, but small improvements in customer experience can help both groups.



CORRELATION OF HEAT MAP:-

1.What Heatmap Represents:
• This chart shows how different things are related to each other.
• If two things increase or decrease together, the value is close to +1 (strong positive relationship).
• If one thing goes up while the other goes down, the value is close to –1 (strong negative relationship).
• If there’s no clear connection, the value is around 0.
• The diagonal is all 1 because each thing is being compared with itself, so it’s always perfectly related.
